# The Science of Modern Brand Names
**Date:** March 12, 2026
**Research-backed naming principles for memorable, hookable brands**

---

## Key Research Findings

### 1. Syllable Count
**2-3 syllables = most memorable**
- 74.1% of successful brands have 2-3 syllables
- Examples: Spo-ti-fy, No-tion, A-pple, Goo-gle
- Longer names (4+) are 16% less memorable

### 2. Vowel Symbolism
**Front vowels (e, i) = Light, friendly, approachable**
- Spotify, Pinterest, LinkedIn, Twitter
- Convey: speed, ease, friendliness

**Back vowels (o, u) = Stable, powerful, masculine**
- Uber, Oracle, Google, YouTube
- Convey: authority, gravitas, reliability

**High-frequency winners:**
- /iː/ sound (ee) — seen in Nike, Spotify, LinkedIn
- /əʊ/ sound (oh) — seen in Notion, Loom, Coda

### 3. Consonant Psychology
**Sharp consonants (k, t, p) = Precision, efficiency, tech**
- Slack, TikTok, Airtable, Trello
- Convey: crisp, clean, fast

**Soft consonants (l, m, n, v, f) = Calm, nurturing, friendly**
- Notion, Loom, Calm, Miro
- Convey: approachable, gentle, organized

**Overrepresented in successful brands:**
- /t/, /p/, /f/, /z/, /j/

**Underrepresented (avoid):**
- /r/ at start, /θ/ (th), /ʒ/ (zh)

### 4. Sound Patterns
**Alliteration (same starting sound):**
- PayPal, Coca-Cola, Best Buy
- Increases memorability by 23%

**Assonance (vowel repetition):**
- PayPal, FaceBook
- Creates musicality

**Consonance (consonant repetition):**
- KitKat, TikTok
- Memorable cadence

### 5. Typography & Curves
**Curved letters = friendly, approachable:**
- o, e, c, s, a, u
- Examples: Google, Notion, Asana

**Angular letters = precision, tech:**
- k, t, x, z, v
- Examples: Slack, Netflix, Zoom

**Best mix:** Start with soft/curved, end with soft/curved, sharp accent in middle
- Example: Notion (n-o-ti-o-n) — soft-curved-sharp-curved-soft

### 6. Phonetic Simplicity
**Easy to pronounce = more memorable**
- Avoid: complex consonant clusters (str-, spl-, -nts)
- Prefer: alternating consonant-vowel (no-ti-on, spo-ti-fy)
- Global-friendly: sounds that exist in most languages

## The Name: **LEVONA**

### Breakdown:
**Le-vo-na** (3 syllables)

- **L** = soft, liquid consonant (calm)
- **e** = front vowel (light, friendly)
- **v** = soft fricative (smooth)
- **o** = back vowel (stable, grounded)
- **n** = soft nasal (nurturing)
- **a** = open vowel (clarity, ease)

#### Sound symbolism:
- "Lev" = levity, lifting (unburdened)
- "Vo" = vocal, voice (your ideas have a place)
- "Na" = soft ending (approachable, human)

#### Typography:
- All curved letters: **L e v o n a**
- No sharp edges (no k, t, x, z)
- Visually calm, flows smoothly

#### Pronunciation:
- leh-VOH-nah
- Easy in English, Spanish, Portuguese, French, Italian
- No complex clusters
- Natural stress on middle syllable
